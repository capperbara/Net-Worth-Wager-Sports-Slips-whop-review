## Understanding Sports Betting Types Covered

  

Before diving deeper into Net Worth Wager's offerings, it's helpful to understand the main types of sports bets they cover:

  

### Primary Betting Categories

  

**Moneyline Bets**

These are straightforward bets on which team will win outright.

The favored team pays lower odds while the underdog offers better payouts.

Net Worth Wager frequently includes moneyline selections in their parlay combinations.

  

**Point Spread Betting**

This involves betting against the spread where bookmakers handicap one team.

For example, if the Lakers are -5.5 against the Warriors, they need to win by 6 or more points.

This is one of the most common bet types in American sports betting.

  

**Over/Under (Total) Bets**

These wagers are based on the combined total score of both teams.

If a basketball game has a total of 220.5, you bet whether the combined score goes over or under.

Net Worth Wager often incorporates these into their daily picks.

  

**Parlay Bets**

Multiple bets combined into one wager for higher payouts.

All selections must win for the parlay to cash.

This is Net Worth Wager's specialty - they focus heavily on professionally curated parlays.

  

**Proposition Bets**

Specific outcome wagers not related to the final score.

Examples include player stats, first team to score, or specific game events.

These are popular for live betting opportunities.

  

**Futures Bets**

Long-term wagers like championship winners or season win totals.

These typically offer higher payouts but tie up money for extended periods.

Net Worth Wager occasionally shares futures picks during major seasons.

  

**Live Betting**

Real-time wagering during games as odds change.

This requires quick decision-making and is where experienced handicappers shine.

The community provides live updates and opportunities throughout games.

## Legal Considerations for Sports Betting

  

### Current Legal Status in the United States

  

Sports betting legality varies significantly by state and jurisdiction.

The Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act (PASPA) previously made sports betting illegal nationwide except in a few grandfathered states.

  

In 2018, the Supreme Court struck down PASPA in Murphy v. National Collegiate Athletic Association.

This opened the door for individual states to legalize sports betting.

  

### Current State of Legalization

  

As of January 2024, **37 states, Washington D.C., and Puerto Rico** have operational legalized sports betting.

North Carolina has also legalized it with operations beginning later in 2024.

  

**30 states, Washington D.C., and Puerto Rico** have legalized online sports betting.

However, 8 states only allow betting through physical sportsbooks.

  

Since the Supreme Court decision, over **$20 billion** has been spent on legal sports betting in the United States as of May 2020.
